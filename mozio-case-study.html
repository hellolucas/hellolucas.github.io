IMPROVING THE TRAVEL EXPERIENCE WITH MOZIO

ABSTRACT

I worked remotely with Mozio for over 1 year.

During this time, I helped redesign their website user experience and interface. This lead to an increase in booking conversions by 90%+ over a 3-month period after launch.

Parallel, we designed and developed their iOS and Android apps from concept to launch, within 6 months, with an average rating of 4 stars.

A WYSIWYG Whitelabel Tool was developed for the Sales & Partnership teams. This helped close partnerships faster, free developer resources, and improved setup time from ~3.5 hours to ~10 minutes.

INTRODUCTION

Mozio is a Search & Compare app that lets you find all types of ground transportation, and book the one that makes the most sense for your wallet and schedule.

Mozio believe the travel experience should be simple and enjoyable. They place the user experience above all else.

GOALS

Mozio wanted to improve booking conversions and increase the types of use cases they could serve. They decided to re-design the user experience and interface of the main website.

Mozio also wanted to increase their direct bookings with users. So they began design and development of an iOS and Android app that could get them closer to travelers.

MY ROLE

I first joined Mozio as a mid-weight UX/UI designer. Together with Steve Ladanyi (Lead Designer) we formed a 2-man team that could serve a 55+ co-workers working remotely. We later were joined by Federico Sarria (UI & Illustration). My role was focused in:

  • Understanding our users
  • Developing use cases
  • Shaping them into user flows
  • Wireframes
  • Prototypes
  • ...and finding different solutions for new features

Being a small team also meant my role was fluid, and I would adapt based on the work at hand. This included interface design and some front-end coding.

Over the course of 1 year, this role evolved from supporting to overseeing the main website redesign and other internal tools.

REDESIGNING AN EXISTING WEBSITE

GETTING STARTED

At start, a few things were already in place: Steve had already began designing a new visual style and there was already a lot of user feedback to work with, as well as several well understood use cases.

We began by reviewing these use cases and collaborating with the new UI design.

I formalised our user into personas and structured the available research so we'd make sure use cases were being designed for in full, ensure a functional redesign that would improve conversions.

User personas (simplified)

RESEARCHING USERS AND EXPANDING USE CASES

User feedback and insight often came from Customer Support, Sales and Partnership teams. They were in a position to identify new problems and opportunities, from:

  • Complaint analysis (Customer support and Review websites)
  • Email surveys on Post-purchase
  • In-depth interviews

This was put together with other research, including:

  • Qualitative questionnaires
  • Booking flow through Google analytics
  • Prototype usability testing

Research was not exclusive to designers and this meant some advantages: Understanding users faster and cheaper, in a greater range of use cases, and in live environments.

From this body of research, issues were wide. Some examples included:

Unclear pricing & Ride time Users often wonder if price is per passenger, or per ride. They also don't know how long the ride is to decide.
Confusion about pickup times Users going To/From an airport ride don't know what should be their pickup times.
Uncertainty about driver reliability First time users are uncertain if the Driver will be arriving on time.

Insights was raw, unstructured and spread, however. Together with the product owners, we made sense of the information and began designing solutions prioritised on the task complexity vs. business and user impact:

Dozens of use cases were designed for, resulting in many new features and improvements.

DEVELOPING THE USER INTERFACE (UI)

Redesigning Mozio’s website UI was an incremental process.

We split design mockups into components that could be assigned to individual developers.

For easier future development, these were designed to be reusable and responsive, and put together in a React component library.

WORKING AGILE: OVERSEEING DESIGN-TO-DEVELOPMENT

Being an UX/UI designer in a company where half of the co-workers are developers means needing to speak both languages.

We worked in weekly sprints, following the agile method. Product managers would assign features & UI components to individual developers, and I would review and support this implementation.

This meant juggling several distinct use cases at any given time, and provide support in the form of:

  • Styleguide specifications such as sizes, colors, states, etc
  • Interactive user flows on how components worked
  • Component behaviours and responses on different interactions
  • Covering use cases not initially considered
  • Adapting the design according to time and tech restrictions
  • Providing icons and visual assets

THE OUTCOME

Mozio platform had it's booking conversion rates increase ~90% over a period of 3 months.

It also achieved:

  • Complete interface overhaul, beginning a new era for Mozio
  • Front-end component library that could be reused for faster development
  • Greatly improved main use cases
  • A framework that would allow to begin designing for new use cases — Train & Bus bookings, different schedules, flight types, ...

With this new acquired growth, Mozio went on to successfully close a new Series round.

WORKING INTERNAL PROJECTS

Improvements were also done internally.

One way in how Mozio operates is by upselling their services in partnerships with Travel and Hotel booking websites. You know after you book a plane ticket, it asks if you want to also book your taxi?

This is done with the use of a Mozio whitelabel that can be styled in line with each partners brand.

How Whitelabel will look like for Mozio partners

To create these, both designer and developer work was required. This meant was Sales & Partnership teams would often have to wait for availability, hindering their ability to pitch and serve potential clients.

And developers could not give full focus in building the main products.

They also required an average of 3.5~ hours to go live.

Tasked with the design of an internal tool that could help creating Whitelabels, I began by interviewing our departments on how they worked and how the process could be improved.

A tool to allow Sales team to create Whitelabels by changing 5 different parameters was devised and mocked up.

Whitelabel tool dashboard

This improved Whitelabel creation times from ~3.5 hours to ~10 minutes/each and free’d valuable developer & designer resources.

MEANWHILE, WITH THE MOBILE APP...

GETTING STARTED

There was already groundwork done in the form of ideas and wireframes established by Mozio founders.

Together with previous research in the form of 1-on-1 interviews, we began by discussing these in efforts to expand and list use cases that we could design for. Some of these were, but not limited to:

Cross Web-Mobile Reservation Management The user needed to have a single account and manage his bookings cross-platform
Train & Tram Bookings Different tram systems have different requirements: entry stop, destination, timed or open ride
Destination/No Destination Use Cases The user could now book without a destination

The launch of App could be broken down into 3 phases :

  1. A tool for Mozio customers to manage their bookings
  2. Add on-demand and scheduled rides booking capabilities
  3. Add Airport express line booking — focus on Heathrow Express (London, UK) and SEPTA (Philadelphia, US)

This would allow iterative development and immediate testing. Efforts began with the iOS App, and later adapted for Android.

USE CASES TO USER FLOWS: MOVING TO MOBILE

We started adapting www.mozio.com user flows to the mobile experience and creating new ones specific to mobile, such as:

  • Onboarding
  • Sign in/sign up
  • On-Demand booking (with & without destination)
  • Choosing picking schedule
  • Airport express line booking
  • etc...
User flows examples — Bus & Train conditional, Sign in/Sign up

One of the challenges was to make sure the website-to-mobile was a seamless experience.

So a website-registered user could login on his mobile, have access to all his booking information and get notifications from cross-platform bookings.

My main role working with the mobile app was developing the user experience. I helped make sure all use cases were covered and user flows were airtight, and we could provide a smooth experience to our travellers.

DESIGNING WIREFRAMES

With the user flows approved, developers could begin building the environment and logic. And we could begin Wireframe design to start having a feel for the app.

On-Demand to Pre-booked: Selecting time and day of ride

These wireframes picked cues from the ones provided by Mozio founders. They also helped fine-tune the user flows, in a back-and-forth process.

During this time, we began exploring visual styles for the mobile app — how it could look like.

HIGH FIDELITY MOCKUPS: PUTTING IT TOGETHER

With a visual style approved, UI design could begin.

The process was iterative and ran parallel to user flow, wireframing and development.

Working tasks & projects in parallel sprints was a common theme. We had to make sure nobody was the bottleneck and developers would not be stuck waiting for design.

As final mockups came out, we began testing for their ease of use, affordance and usability.

We did this by creating rapid prototypes with InVision/Proto.io and running usability tests with Mozio co-workers, as well as on the streets of San Francisco.

This gave much needed feedback and kept the design process going.
